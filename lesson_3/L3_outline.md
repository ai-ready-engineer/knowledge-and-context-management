# Lesson 3 — Knowledge Graphs

**Number:** L3
**Tagline:** Facts in isolation are trivia. A graph of entities and relations is the structure that lets you navigate, reason, and see what's missing — the target we'll populate, query, and mine.
**Lifecycle stage:** Connect (the structure we build before we fill it)
**Quality dimension in focus:** Completeness & consistency

## What we cover
- Anatomy of a knowledge graph: entities, relations, properties, and the schema/ontology on top
- RDF triples vs. labeled property graphs; a first look at SPARQL and Cypher
- Why connection is its own representation decision — a graph is the structure the next three lessons depend on
- KG vs. vector store — relational/multi-hop questions vs. "find similar"
- When a knowledge graph is worth the cost — and when it is not

## Skills you unlock
- Model a domain as entities and relations
- Read and write basic graph queries (SPARQL / Cypher)
- Decide between a graph, a vector store, or both for a use case
- See coverage gaps as missing edges and contradictions as conflicting edges
- Design the schema that extraction (L4) will populate

## Tools and examples
Interactive graph builder (model a domain live) · query playground (Cypher/SPARQL) · graph-vs-vector comparison on relational questions · a small ontology you design for the support domain.
