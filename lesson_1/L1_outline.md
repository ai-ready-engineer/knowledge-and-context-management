# Lesson 1 — Quality of Knowledge in the Age of AI

**Number:** L1
**Tagline:** Which "types" of Enterprise Knowlege do we have? Who consumes and produces it? What makes knowledge *good*, and how do we measure it?


## What we cover
- Data → information → knowledge: a useful distinction, and where the tidy DIKW pyramid misleads
- The dimensions of knowledge quality: correctness, completeness/coverage, consistency, freshness, relevance, provenance, findability, actionability
- How to measure each dimension — gold questions, answerability, citation correctness, contradiction rate, staleness
- Why, in the age of AI, knowledge-base quality sets the *ceiling* on every answer the AI gives (garbage in, garbage out)
- Building a knowledge-quality scorecard you can actually track over time

## Skills you unlock
- Name the quality dimensions that matter for your domain, and weight them
- Turn "is our knowledge good?" into a measurable scorecard
- Trace a bad AI answer back to the knowledge defect behind it
- Set a "good enough to ship" bar for a knowledge base
- Ask the question that exposes a knowledge base's weak point

## Tools and examples
Knowledge-quality scorecard template · gold-question / answerability evaluation · contradiction-rate and staleness probes.
