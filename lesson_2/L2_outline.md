# Lesson 2 — Knowledge Representation

**Number:** L2
**Tagline:** How you store knowledge decides what you can ever do with it. Text, schemas, taxonomies, triples, embeddings — each opens some doors and closes others.
**Lifecycle stage:** Represent
**Quality dimension in focus:** Findability & consistency

## What we cover
- The representation spectrum: unstructured text → semi-structured → structured (tables, taxonomies, ontologies, triples) → vector/embedding representations
- Symbolic vs. sub-symbolic knowledge — what each makes easy and what each makes impossible
- Taxonomies, ontologies, and schemas: the shared vocabulary that makes knowledge interoperable
- Chunking and embeddings for RAG — what gets captured, what gets lost
- Hybrid representations, and choosing one for a concrete use case

## Skills you unlock
- Pick a representation that fits the questions you need to answer
- Sketch a taxonomy or lightweight schema for a domain
- Reason about what an embedding representation can and cannot retrieve
- Combine representations instead of forcing everything into one
- Spot when the representation — not the model — is the bottleneck

## Tools and examples
Representation explorer (same facts as text, table, triples, and embeddings) · chunking-and-embedding visualizer · a small taxonomy you build live.
